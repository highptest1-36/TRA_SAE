Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "TRA-SAE: A Low-Cost
Retrieval-Augmented Expert Reasoning System for Mixed Physics-Logic Question
Answering with a 4B Language Model" for consideration as an Original Article in
Expert Systems.

Our work concerns the design of an expert reasoning system for scientific
question answering that couples numerical physical reasoning with symbolic
logical inference under strict resource constraints. Using a single
4B-parameter open model and a total compute budget below US$64, we develop and
rigorously evaluate a curriculum training pipeline -- supervised fine-tuning, a
logic-focused curriculum stage, and Group Relative Policy Optimization with a
composite reward -- combined with retrieval-augmented inference and an optional
symbolic (Z3) verification component.

We believe the manuscript fits the scope of Expert Systems in the following
respects. First, it concerns knowledge-based and reasoning systems that
integrate machine learning with symbolic verification. Second, it provides a
transparent, reproducible methodology with full statistical analysis (McNemar
tests, Wilson confidence intervals, and a three-seed evaluation) under a clean
evaluation protocol that does not use ground-truth labels at inference time.
Third, it reports actionable and honestly characterised findings -- including
negative results -- for practitioners who build scientific-reasoning systems on
a limited budget.

The principal contributions are: (i) a low-cost, reproducible pipeline for
mixed-domain (physics and logic) scientific question answering on a small open
model; (ii) a controlled causal decomposition showing that retrieval-augmented
exemplars, rather than fine-tuning, account for the larger share of the accuracy
gain; (iii) a fair multi-prompt baseline re-evaluation and external transfer
checks on MMLU physics and FOLIO logic; and (iv) a structured error analysis
that identifies formal logical inference as the principal bottleneck at this
model scale, with the measured gains concentrated in the physics domain.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published previously,
and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have
approved the submission and declare no conflicts of interest. The code, trained
adapters, evaluation scripts, the exact train/validation split, and the raw
per-sample predictions are available in an anonymised repository for review, and
a permanent, citable archive will be deposited upon acceptance.
